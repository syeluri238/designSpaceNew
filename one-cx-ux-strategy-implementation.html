Enhancing Customer Experience and Reducing Returns for a Garage Door Manufacturer

The Brief

Our client is one of America’s largest residential garage door manufacturers in the commercial and industrial door industry. For decades, our client’s brand has stood for product quality and expertise.

Significant barriers

  • Legacy application developed with page-based approach.
  • Application is scattered into many pages causing absence of journey with no subsequent updates.
  • The Application is not interacting with the user as per the latest user experience guidelines.
  • Users are investing more than desired time & effort to perform any task, which is in turn impacting client’s business.
  • Application lacks intuitiveness, empathy and has no learning curve.

Satisfied customers, successful business

Business goals

To create experiences leveraging goal direction design method based on user motivation and objectives.

  • Enhance the ease of use by simplifying the application.
  • Introduce the guided approach to help a user walk through a sequence of tasks on the way toward achievingthat goal.
  • Group users and define their roles and activities.
  • Define the notification system keeping all users updated and informed.

Course of action

To give the user a smooth experience, we adhered to the 4D method. Four distinct phases make up the process. Our efforts started with doing a thorough analysis of the company, users, and the target audience, understanding their pain concerns, and carefully observing the workplace environment.

Stakeholders Analysis

Discussions were held with stakeholders to get the glance of the business, tools, various roles to drive business,

  • What are the important area to be addressed properly?
  • What kind of changes they are envisioning ?

Later, we talked to the domain experts to understand the problem space better. For instance, if we design the interface used by business professionals, we need to know the important steps of the process, the context, under what circumstances is it going to be used and so on.

Questionnaire
  • What will be different one year after this project is completed?
  • Which of these problems is the most important to solve?
  • In what ways do you see the project helping you achieve your mission?
  • What happens if this project is not done?
  • What tangible changes do you expect to see at the completion of this project?
  • What are the major risks to the project’s success?
  • How will you know if this project has been successfully completed?
  • What are the major obstacles or impediments to the success of this project?

User Personal

Who are they?
  • CTO, CFO, Account Manager, Product Owner, and others
  • Age: 35-60 Years
  • Gender: Male and female
  • Family: Plural
  • Education: Degree, PG
Main Goals/What do they want?
  • To learn new tools and technology for to serve better and improve work efficiency.
  • Make the decision better based on data available.
Pain-points/Frustration?
  • Needs to take and make decision quickly because time is very important factor.
  • It takes much time to complete small task, there is not task mechanism approach.
  • Lacking Good quality of data
BIO

Executive level role, Responsible for overall business strategy leading sales, marketing, product development, finance, and customer service activities to drive growth in residential and commercial business segments

Delivered Enhanced business value

Metrics are the signals that show whether the UX strategy is working. Using these metrics, we tracked changes overtime, benchmarked against iterations of application. Here’re the key business benefits.

  • Improved overall app accessibility and usability in a very short period.
  • Low overall bounce rate & healthy average time on app indicated good visitor retention and user satisfaction.
  • Response rate is now faster than the previous application.
  • Reduced time consumption to complete an overall task.
  • Task mechanism helped raise the productivity.
